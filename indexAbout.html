ABOUT

Keyboardist | Singer | Songwriter

"I’ve always identified as a musician more than anything, and I figured you should do what you love..." - Rogue Johnsen

Rogue Johnsen learned his craft on the road, backing up blues recording artists out of Oakland and Austin. The only student of the late, great Charles Brown, he’s been a sideman for everyone from Bo Diddley to Archie Bell and the Drells.

In 2016, he released two CD’s of his original music: Way Back and Trouble Blues. Way Back, co-produced with Merle Brigante, is Rogue’s band work from Austin, Texas; Trouble Blues was recorded solo, “live in the studio,” in early 2016. Combining his blues roots with an early love of psychedelic rock, Rogue Johnsen writes well-crafted and thoughtful songs that come from the soul.

Rogue Johnsen has performed and studied and with some amazing musicians,: artists whose roots dig deep into the history of Rock and Blues. In addition to studying with Chuck Israels, Tony Nalker and Ron Thompson, Rogue holds the rare distinction of being the only student of the great Charles Brown.

Having toured extensively with W.C. Clark, Archie Bell & the Drells, and Miss Lavelle White, Rogue’s also been a sideman for such legends as Bo Diddley, Johnny Adams, Norton Buffalo, Larry “Arkansas” Davis, Sam Andrew and Will Scarlet.

He’s been the opening act for B.B. King, Albert King, Earl King, Buddy Guy, Bobby Bland, James Cotton, Maceo Parker, Luther Allison, Dave Mason, Koko Taylor, Robben Ford, A. C. Reed, Syl Johnson, The Blind Boys of Alabama, and The Gospel Hummingbirds.

These artists represent an incredible scope of knowledge and influences, and they permeate Johnsen’s songwriting and playing. Adding some uniquely well-crafted lyrics to the sound he’s developed, and Rogue Johnsen somehow makes it feel new, while still taking you way back.
